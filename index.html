Hi folks, this is my personal academic page. :)

I was born in Podgorica, Montenegro, in November 1987. I received a BSc degree in electrical engineering from the University of Montenegro (2009) and an MSc degree in optics and RF engineering from Grenoble INP (2011). In 2014, after working at GIPSA-lab in Grenoble, I earned a PhD in remote sensing from L’Université Grenoble-Alpes and simultaneously a PhD from the University of Montenegro. I then served as a postdoctoral researcher at the Environmental Remote Sensing Laboratory (EPFL) in Lausanne, also collaborating with the Radar, Satellites, and Nowcasting group (MeteoSwiss) in Locarno. My career also includes research positions at the Centre for Radar Meteorology (Météo-France) in Toulouse and at AgroParisTech in Nancy, before joining the French National Mapping Agency (IGN).

My research primarily focuses on remote sensing and, increasingly, on the modeling of the cryosphere, atmosphere, and biosphere. Broadly, my research interests encompass statistical signal and image processing, electromagnetism, and machine learning/data mining techniques, all closely tied to Earth sciences and particularly to the challenges posed by climate change.
